FIG. 01 — Active Research

Scalable Synthesis of Nanoparticles: Metal-Organic Frameworks

Seattle, WA · Oct 2025 – Present

I'm developing a continuous synthesis process for metal-organic frameworks and their composites using supercritical CO₂, evaluating how dilution factor, injection temperature, reactor pressure, and residence time affect yield, particle size, and material quality. In parallel, I'm designing and optimizing an automated microdevice for single-cell lipid assays, integrating CAD, microcontrollers, and electrical/mechanical prototyping.

FIG. 02 — Capstone

Senior Design Capstone: Applied Materials

Austin, TX · Jan – May 2024

A team of 5 of us tackled two shop-floor problems for Applied Materials: operators losing time walking to gather hardware, and loose tubing reels creating disorder lineside. I ran cycle-time studies and operator interviews, mapped walking patterns and bottlenecks, then prototyped and FEA-tested solutions in SolidWorks against human-factor extremes. Our recommendation — adding a dedicated hardware-runner role — saved an estimated $40,000 a year and scales across facilities.

France / Greece / US · Aug – Dec 2023

Working with 3 engineers across 2 universities and sponsored by Aristotle University of Thessaloniki, I helped plan the engineering and international-business case for a 3D-printed meat venture, then presented and defended the proposal to professors from three universities.

FIG. 04 — Thesis

Carbon Capture, Storage & Utilization (CCSU)

Lyngby, Denmark · Jan – May 2022

With a 4-person international team, I designed a CCSU retrofit for an existing high-emissions cement plant, comparing partial oxy-fuel combustion, amine scrubbing, and calcium looping in AutoCAD. We also modeled the impact of introducing carbon-removal credits into Denmark's energy infrastructure. Our calcium-looping design — presented and defended to industry experts — cut plant CO₂ emissions by more than 75%.

FIG. 05 — Thesis

Robust Design of Products & Mechanisms

Lyngby, Denmark · Jan – May 2022

A 7-person international team and I improved the reliability of a 3D laser printer's powder-deposition mechanism using robust design methodology — reducing sensitivity to variation without tightening costly tolerances. I applied graph-based modeling for tolerance specification and process-capability prediction, then used CAD/CAM to redesign and test the mechanism.

FIG. 06 — Thesis

Energy Systems: Design, Analysis & Optimization

Lyngby, Denmark · Jan – May 2022

My team of 4 modeled four energy system types — power plants, combined heat & power, heat pumps, and refrigeration systems — using pinch and exergy analysis to weigh future technology trade-offs. I helped optimize designs with exergoeconomic analysis in EES, DNA, and Python, and built dynamic models for instantaneous operating strategy.
